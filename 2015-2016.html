Where it starts.

In the first year i was inspired by a stone. I found him ten years ago in a little village in Germany.
The first part of the year i painted the stone himself. In the second part i wanted to make a landscape with stones, not just the stone himself.

I was surprised by the oil paint myself. I usually paint with watercolor and I can play with water and pigment. I tried to play the same way with the oil paint colors, so it became paintings that could not dry. I took pictures because the painting itself no longer exists. What I found very interesting in the paintings was the atmosphere, it was really cosmic.

Actually it's the floor you're looking at now.
I painted against the wall. Due to the large amounts of oil the paint flowed through the wall to the floor. This makes the floor part of the painting

So the floor became my painting. It was seen on my jurry and I was very pleased with the work, but due to the long drying time, the work could not continue and destroyed itself once I rolled it up.
